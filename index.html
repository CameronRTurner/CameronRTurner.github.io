Cameron Rouse Turner

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Princeton University, where I work with Tom Griffiths on using formalisms from artificial intelligence to study the evolution and diversity of animal cognition. My research asks how evolution shapes learning and intelligence across species, combining mathematical modelling, reinforcement learning, and comparative psychology.


I received my PhD from the Australian National University, where I developed evolutionary models of how organisms use environmental information — from alarm calls to social learning. I also hold scholarships from St Andrews and the Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Anthropology. My work spans evolutionary biology, cognitive science, and philosophy of science, and has been published in venues including Proceedings of the Royal Society B, Behavioral Ecology and Sociobiology, and the Cognitive Science Society.
